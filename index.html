Marinduque

Marinduque is an island province in the Mimaropa region that sits between Tayabas Bay and the Sibuyan Sea. It is located in the heart of the Philippines and is considered the geographic center of the archipelago. During the Lenten season, the island becomes quite crowded for the annual Moriones Festival, one of the Philippines' oldest religious festivals.

Marinduque, with its idyllic beaches and caves, has several must-see attractions that draw tourists during the Lenten season and the summer months.
